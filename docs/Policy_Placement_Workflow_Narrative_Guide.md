# Policy Placement Workflow: Structure and Coordination Guide

## Executive Summary

This document provides a comprehensive overview of the Advantage annuity structure with SMA deployment under ALPR ownership, managed by Syndicated Capital. The workflow is designed to ensure regulatory compliance, operational clarity, and clean role separation throughout the case placement and ongoing management process.

**Three distinct phases govern this workflow:**
- **Phase 0**: Pre-Application Coordination
- **Phase 1**: Broker Role (Case Placement)  
- **Phase 2**: RIA Role (SMA Management)

Between Phase 1 and Phase 2, a **Transition Window** (~3 business days) allows Advantage to complete SMA infrastructure setup while maintaining complete separation between broker and RIA activities.

---

## Foundational Principles

### Role Separation
The workflow maintains strict separation between broker dealer activities (case placement) and registered investment advisor activities (portfolio management). This separation is critical for:
- Regulatory compliance
- Compensation structure clarity
- Conflict of interest avoidance
- 817(h) diversification rule adherence

### No-Client-Control Framework
The SMA operates under a discretionary management agreement where:
- ALPR owns the policy
- ALPR grants discretionary authority to Syndicated Capital
- The client has no direct control over investment decisions
- All allocations maintain compliance with IRC Section 817(h)

### Administrative Access Structure
Advantage does not use a traditional "broker of record" process. Instead, **ALPR grants administrative access to Syndicated Capital** through the discretionary management agreement. This provides all functional capabilities needed for RIA management without blending brokerage and advisory roles.

## Phase 1: Broker Role (Case Placement)

**Duration**: Variable, typically 2-6 weeks

**Primary Actor**: Stephen through Broker Dealer

**Oversight**: Faith (B/D Compliance Officer)

### Six-Step Process

**Step 1**: Application Signed
- Stephen executes application through Broker Dealer
- All required documentation submitted to Advantage

**Step 2**: Policy In Force
- Advantage underwrites and approves application
- Policy issued and placed in force

**Step 3**: Premium Wired
- Client wires premium payment to Advantage
- Funds received and acknowledged

**Step 4**: Administrative Fee Deducted
- Advantage deducts administrative fee from premium
- Fee structure per policy terms

**Step 5**: Commission Paid
- Advantage pays commission to Broker Dealer
- Commission processed per agreement

**Step 6**: Broker Role Ends
- All broker responsibilities fully terminate
- No ongoing commissions
- No renewal obligations
- Clean separation achieved

### Support Roles During Phase 1
- **Faith**: B/D compliance oversight for Stephen
- **TPBC**: Coordination support
- **3cStructures**: Technical support for Advantage
- **Advantage**: Application processing and administrative support

### Phase 1 Outcome
✓ Case successfully placed and funded  
✓ Broker responsibilities completely concluded  
✓ Process enters transition window

## Key Distinctions and Clarifications

### Broker vs. RIA Activities

**Broker Dealer (Phase 1 ONLY)**
- Case placement and application execution
- Commission-based compensation
- Ends completely after funding and commission payment
- NO ongoing relationship
- NO renewals
- NO trail commissions

**RIA (Phase 2 ONLY)**
- Discretionary portfolio management
- Fee-based compensation (via SMA structure)
- Begins only after transition window
- Ongoing fiduciary relationship
- Continuous oversight and management

### Administrative Access vs. Broker of Record

**Traditional Broker of Record**
- Ongoing broker relationship
- Commission renewals possible
- Potential role confusion
- NOT USED in this structure

**Administrative Access (This Structure)**
- Granted by ALPR to Syndicated Capital
- Provides functional portfolio management capabilities
- No broker relationship implied
- Clean separation maintained
- Supports RIA fiduciary role only

### The Critical Separation

The transition window ensures complete temporal separation between broker and RIA roles. This separation is essential for:
- Regulatory compliance (no dual registration conflicts)
- Compensation clarity (commission vs. fee separation)
- Fiduciary duty clarity (placement vs. management)
- Conflict avoidance (product sale vs. advisory service)

## Compliance and Regulatory Framework

### IRC Section 817(h) Compliance
The SMA maintains diversification requirements under IRC Section 817(h) to preserve the tax treatment of the variable life insurance contract. All allocations are monitored and adjusted as needed to maintain compliance.

### No-Client-Control Rule
The client does not direct investment decisions. ALPR owns the policy and grants discretionary authority to Syndicated Capital. This structure ensures the annuity maintains its proper tax treatment.

### Fiduciary Standards
Syndicated Capital (Stephen as RIA) acts as a fiduciary during Phase 2, with obligations to:
- Act in the best interest of the client
- Provide ongoing portfolio monitoring
- Maintain appropriate diversification
- Disclose any conflicts of interest

### Oversight and Compliance Monitoring
- **Faith**: Compliance officer oversight throughout all phases
- **Advantage**: Policy-level compliance and valuation monitoring
- **TPBC**: Coordination oversight and process monitoring
